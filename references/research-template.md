# Research Template

## Research Protocol

Run 5-15 web searches per event. Cross-reference at minimum 3 sources for any single claimed fact.

### Search Strategy
1. Start broad: event name + location + date
2. Narrow to specifics: named individuals, physical locations, evidence, official statements
3. Cross-check: alternative sources, official agency statements, local media vs national media
4. Identify disputes: where sources conflict, note both versions
5. Detail hunt: search specifically for the physical environment, business names, staff names, prices, signage, layout, weather, what people were wearing

### Extraction Categories

For every event, extract the following. Leave blank if not available. Mark disputed items.

#### Geographic Data
- Primary location (address or area, coordinates if relevant)
- Surrounding area type (urban/suburban/rural, density, terrain)
- Secondary locations (any other location connected to the event)
- Distances between locations
- Major transit routes or escape routes
- Public vs restricted access boundaries

#### Timeline
- Last confirmed normal state (before the event)
- Estimated event onset
- First notification to authorities
- First public awareness
- Each subsequent development with date and time
- Current status

#### People
For each person or group:
- Name (or designation if unnamed)
- Role in the event
- What they have publicly stated (direct quotes with source)
- What they control (information, access, resources, narrative)
- What they do not control
- Current status

#### Physical Evidence
For each item:
- What it is
- Where it was found
- Who found it
- Current custody
- Forensic status (tested, pending, results)

#### Communications
- Official statements (by whom, when, what channel)
- Media reports (which outlets, what they reported, any conflicts between reports)
- Public appeals
- Any demands or notes
- Digital communications

#### Time-Sensitive Factors
- Medical urgency
- Evidence degradation risk
- Legal deadlines
- Processing delays (lab work, warrants, etc.)
- Media cycle pressure

#### Known vs Unknown
- Confirmed facts (with source)
- Confirmed absences (what has NOT been found, what has NOT happened)
- Operational unknowns (things investigators are working on)
- Public speculation (clearly label as speculation, not endorsed)
- Disputed facts (where sources conflict)

### Detail Harvesting Protocol (V2)

During research, maintain a separate running list of **vivid verified details**. These are the raw materials for immersion in the final thread.

Hunt for:
- **Prices:** What things cost at the locations involved. Menu prices. Cover charges. Drink specials. Entry fees.
- **Signage:** What signs are posted. Warnings. Rules. Business names as they appear on the building.
- **Physical layout:** How rooms are arranged. What is in the corners. What is on the walls. Where the doors are. How big or small the space is.
- **Objects:** Specific items in the environment. Furniture. Equipment. Vehicles. Fixtures.
- **Names:** Real names of staff, witnesses, officials who have gone on record. Business names. Street names. Neighborhood names.
- **Clothing and appearance:** What people were wearing, if reported. Tattoos. Accessories. Physical descriptions from witnesses.
- **Weather and time:** What the weather was. What time of day. What the light was like. What season.
- **Sounds:** What was audible. Music. Traffic. Crowds. Sirens. Silence.
- **Routine details:** What normally happens at this location. Regular events. Schedules. Traditions. Monday specials. Operating hours.
- **Distances:** How far apart things are. Walking time. Driving time. Blocks.

Format each detail as a single line with source:
```
- "The bar has at least fifty Cash Only signs" (Source: Hollywood Reporter, bartender Kyle Catarouch)
- "$4 High Lifes" (Source: R Bar website)
- "Haircut and a shot for ten dollars on Mondays" (Source: multiple reviews, Hollywood Reporter)
```

**Why this matters:** A verified detail does more work than a paragraph of invented atmosphere. "$4 High Lifes" places the reader in the bar. "A dimly lit establishment" does not. The real is always more vivid than the imagined.

### Output Format

Organize research as a flat document with clear section headers. No narrative prose. Bullet points with source attribution. Every claimed fact must have at least one source.

Mark confidence levels:
- **CONFIRMED:** Multiple independent sources agree
- **REPORTED:** Single credible source, not independently verified
- **DISPUTED:** Sources conflict
- **UNKNOWN:** No information available
- **SPECULATION:** Public or media conjecture, not supported by official sources
