# Board Components

## Format Specifications

Every component is a structured entry. No prose. No narrative. No interpretation.

---

## LOCATIONS (L1 to Ln)

Each location entry:

```
**L[n]: [Name]**
- Type: [private residence / commercial / public space / government facility / digital platform / etc.]
- Access level: [public / restricted / law-enforcement only / digital only / military only]
- Known movement: [Who went there, when, what happened there. Chronological, factual only.]
- Verified details: [Physical details from research: layout, signage, fixtures, prices, operating hours, neighborhood context]
```

Rules:
- Include every distinct physical or digital location mentioned in research
- Digital locations count (social media accounts, cryptocurrency wallets, communication platforms)
- Access level describes who CAN access, not who HAS accessed
- Known movement is strictly what is documented, not inferred
- Verified details field is new in V2. Capture every physical detail available from research. These will fuel immersive writing in moves.

---

## ACTORS (A1 to An)

Each actor entry:

```
**A[n]: [Name or Designation]**
- Controls: [What information, resources, movement, narrative, access they hold]
- Does not control: [What they cannot influence]
- Public visibility: [low / medium / high / extreme]
- Verified details: [Physical description, clothing, identifying features, direct quotes attributed to them in reporting]
```

Rules:
- List every individual and group with agency in the event
- Include institutional actors (agencies, media organizations, companies)
- "Controls" means operational control, not desire or intent
- Do NOT infer motive from control
- Visibility is about public awareness of their role, not their physical visibility
- Include named individuals who have gone on record (bartenders, doormen, officials, witnesses)

---

## ARTIFACTS (E1 to En)

Each artifact entry:

```
**E[n]: [Item Name]**
- Location found: [L-reference]
- Chain of custody: [Who found it, who has it, where it went. Chronological.]
- Current status: [In evidence / released / processed / pending / destroyed / unknown]
```

Rules:
- Include every physical or digital item that functions as evidence, leverage, or communication
- Public statements and media appearances count as artifacts (they are records with specific content and timing)
- Social media posts count as artifacts with timestamps
- Video footage counts as artifacts with source attribution
- Chain of custody is what is documented, not assumed
- If status is unknown, say unknown

---

## TICKING CLOCKS (C1 to Cn)

Each clock entry:

```
**C[n]: [Clock Name]**
- Type: [biological / forensic / legal / media / evidence degradation / jurisdictional / political / operational / narrative]
- Description: [One sentence: what is degrading or expiring and why it matters]
```

Rules:
- Every time-sensitive constraint gets its own entry
- Do not rank clocks
- Do not assess which clock is "most important"
- V2 addition: "narrative" clock type. This is for when a public figure's stated narrative (sobriety, reform, redemption) is under time pressure from emerging contradictory evidence. The clock measures how long the gap between stated narrative and documented behavior can hold before public collapse.
- Types of clocks to look for:
  - Biological: health of victim, survival window, medication access
  - Forensic: lab processing time, DNA analysis, autopsy scheduling
  - Evidence degradation: weather effects on outdoor evidence, digital storage rolling overwrite, witness memory decay
  - Legal: warrant expiration, statute limitations, filing deadlines
  - Media: news cycle attention span, public patience, hoax proliferation rate
  - Jurisdictional: handoff timing between agencies, international cooperation delays
  - Political: election cycles, appointment changes, budget pressures
  - Operational: resource allocation limits, personnel rotation, search window closure
  - Narrative: public persona vs documented behavior gap, credibility window, statement timing pressure

---

## ESCALATION BEATS (Beat 1 to n)

Each beat entry:

```
**Beat [n]: [Date]**
- What changed: [One sentence]
- Opened: [What new channel of information, power, evidence, or communication became available]
- Closed: [What hypothesis, assumption, or channel was eliminated]
```

Rules:
- An escalation beat is a moment where the case STRUCTURALLY changed, not where something interesting happened
- Structural change means: a new information channel opened, an old one closed, the type of case changed, the power balance shifted, or the geographic scope expanded or contracted
- Not every development is a beat. A press conference that repeats known information is not a beat. A DNA result that changes who is suspected IS a beat.
- Chronological order only
- One beat per structural shift (do not combine multiple shifts into one beat unless they are causally inseparable)
